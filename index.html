Integrating science and implementation to save our planet and improve health

Our research combines a standardization of food lifecycle analysis (LCA) and behavioral economics to bridge the knowledge-action gap in our food purchasing decisions.

The Challenge

Our individual food choices at the supermarket can change the food system to adopt more sustainable practices and promote better health outcomes.

However, a lack of relevant knowledge at critical moments of purchase are barriers to action.

  • Standardization

    No trusted standard in understanding and measuring the carbon footprint of food consumption.

  • User Preference

    Lack of understanding on what information can lead to behavior change (knowledge-action gap).

  • Relevant Touchpoints

    No relevant information related to carbon footprint readily available at the time of the purchase.
